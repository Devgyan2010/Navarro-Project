Interactive Timeline - Nothing But the Truth

-by Devgyan Prakash

Philip always hated Ms. Narwin, calling her a boring and unfair teacher. He gets really riled up after he fails a test based on a book (Call of the Wild) and gets a D at the end of a term. Now, he can't tryout for track, and is furious.
To his dissapointment, stuck in Ms. Narwin's homeroom, Philip begins to hum the national anthem as it played over the loud speaker even though the teacher reminded him that students must remain silent. Philip defiently continues against all warnings and the relationship between him and the teacher grows further apart.
Philip continued his behavior for two days, and got sent to the office. The second time he gets sent to Dr. Palleni he is threatened with suspension, but given a chance to redeem himself, which he declines. Dr. Palleni has no choice but to give him a two-day suspension.
After his mom picks him up, and once his dad comes back home, Philip explains everything in a way to make it seem his english teacher is the problam, and the school doesn't support patriots. His parents start to believe that the school is being unfair and encourages him to stand up for himself.
Philips dad supports Philip completely and he discusses this situation with their neighbor, Ted Griffen, who is running for school board. He has a reporter over, and asks Philip to tell her everything, and soon, the story gains popularity portraying Philip as a student who was punished for showing patriotism. This turns into a national contreversy.
Local news turns national, and both Ms. Narwin and Philip get lots of attention, and the nation supports Philip and put hate on Ms. Narwin. Though Philip should be happy, he finds out that many of his classmates and close friends are against him and are mad he put blame on Ms. Narwin. Eventually, his parents transfer him to Washington Academy, a private school. In a ironic turn of events, when asked to sing the national anthem for the class, he says... "I don't know the words"
